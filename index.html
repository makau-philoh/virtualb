Topic: To Determine the Relationship Between Work and Power

Theory of the Experiment

THEORETICAL BACKGROUND:
Work can be defined as the energy transfer between objects through some distance. Work is said to be done when a certain amount of force moves an object for a certain distance. It is calculated as the product of force applied and the distance moved in the direction of the force.
Power is the amount of energy transferred or converted per unit time, meaning it refers to the rate at which work is performed.
Work is related to the first law of motion because, to change the state of an object, a force must be applied; if that force causes displacement, work is done.
W = F × D
The second law directly links work with the concept of force. When a force is applied to accelerate an object, the object moves and work is done over the distance it travels.
F = m × a
Additionally, the work-energy theorem states that the net work done on an object is equal to the change in its kinetic energy.
Wnet = ∆KE = KEfinal - KEinitial

Apparatus

  • Mass
  • Meter rule
  • Step/bench
  • Stopwatch

Method

  1. Hold a mass at waist height and step up and down on the bench. Measure the displacement.
  2. Hold masses in each hand and raise from down straight to above the shoulder. Measure the displacement.
  3. Perform squats while holding masses and measure the displacement.
  4. Raise your hand to shoulder level while holding a mass and measure displacement.
